WHO IT’S FOR

People who move and want structured, herbs-only recovery.

Best fit: athletes, coaches, and everyday movers who are already training or working hard and want plant-based routines to support sleep, fatigue, and general recovery—within a clearly non-medical frame.

It is for people who read labels, respect their doctors, and understand that herbs are one part of a larger picture. It works best when paired with disciplined training and sensible rest.

It is a poor fit for people seeking cures, diagnosis, or treatment here, or anyone looking to replace medical care with herbs alone.

What this lane is—and is not.

Health by Texas Movement is educational and systems-focused. It is built to support performance and everyday life, not to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent disease.

All routines are framed as herbs-only support with clear disclaimers. They are meant to be discussed with your medical team, not to replace them. If you have symptoms, conditions, or questions about treatment, talk to a qualified healthcare professional.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Is this medical advice?

No. Health by Texas Movement is educational and systems-focused. It does not diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent disease and does not replace medical advice or care.

What does “herbs-only” mean?

It means the routines focus on plant-based products and behaviors, framed as support for recovery and performance—not as treatment or cure for any condition.

Who should I talk to about symptoms or conditions?

Always talk to a qualified healthcare professional—your doctor, specialist, or nurse. Health by Texas Movement is not a clinic and does not provide care.

Can I stop my medication and just use herbs?

No. Do not change medication or treatment plans based on anything here. Any changes should be made with and under the supervision of your medical team.

How does this connect to Texas Movement Performance?

Health supports Performance with herbs-only recovery systems that sit alongside training blocks. Training and recovery are designed to work together, not compete.

Where should I go for serious or urgent issues?

For serious or urgent issues, seek immediate medical care from your doctor, clinic, or emergency services. This site is not an emergency resource.
